# DDYO Genuine Dipole Cancellation Route

## Eliminated insufficient route

The conditions
\[
\int S_k = 0,
\qquad
\int \omega_k = 0
\]
alone do not imply cancellation of the paired remainder.

## Sole remaining Claim 5 route

The remaining terminal target is to prove that the first-order Taylor contribution in the paired remainder is itself orthogonal to the shell interaction after kernel symmetrization, so that the remainder behaves as a dipole and gains the second factor
\[
2^{-k}.
\]

## Frozen target

For \(|j-k|\le C\),
\[
|\mathsf R_{j,k}|
\le
C\,2^{-j}2^{-k}\,
\|\nabla S_k\|_{L^\infty}\,
\|\omega_k\|_{L^1},
\]
where the first \(2^{-j}\) comes from the kernel first moment and the second \(2^{-k}\) comes from genuine paired dipole / Taylor / kernel cancellation.

## Weakest sufficient structural lemma

It is sufficient to prove that the first-order Taylor piece
\[
\mathsf T^{(1)}_{j,k}
\]
of the paired commutator remainder satisfies
\[
\mathsf T^{(1)}_{j,k}=0
\]
after shell-frequency localization and kernel symmetrization, leaving only a second-order remainder with net gain
\[
2^{-j}2^{-k}.
\]

## Consequence

This is the sole remaining primary route for Claim 5 after elimination of divergence-free strain and pure mean-zero cancellation.
