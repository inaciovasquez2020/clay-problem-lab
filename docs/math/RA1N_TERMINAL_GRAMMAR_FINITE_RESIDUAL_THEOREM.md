# RA1n Terminal Grammar Finite-Residual Theorem

Status: CONDITIONAL STRUCTURAL THEOREM

## Target

Prove the finite residual expansion

\[
(I-\Pi_V)F
=
\sum_{j=1}^m c_j(F)\psi_j
\qquad
\forall F\in\mathcal P_{\mathrm{RA1n}}^{\mathrm{terminal}}.
\]

## Objects

Let

\[
V_{\mathrm{RA1n}}=\operatorname{span}\{\phi_1\}.
\]

Let

\[
W_{\perp}
=
\operatorname{span}\{\psi_1,\dots,\psi_m\}
\subset
V_{\mathrm{RA1n}}^\perp.
\]

Let

\[
\Pi_V:L^2\to V_{\mathrm{RA1n}}
\]

be the orthogonal projection.

## Terminal Grammar Hypothesis

Assume every terminal RA1n packet is generated by the finite grammar

\[
\mathcal G_{\mathrm{terminal}}
=
\{\phi_1,\psi_1,\dots,\psi_m\}
\]

under finite complex linear combination.

Equivalently, for every

\[
F\in\mathcal P_{\mathrm{RA1n}}^{\mathrm{terminal}},
\]

there exist coefficients

\[
a(F),c_1(F),\dots,c_m(F)\in\mathbb C
\]

such that

\[
F
=
a(F)\phi_1
+
\sum_{j=1}^m c_j(F)\psi_j.
\]

## Orthogonality Hypothesis

Assume

\[
\psi_j\in V_{\mathrm{RA1n}}^\perp
\qquad
1\le j\le m.
\]

Thus

\[
\Pi_V\psi_j=0
\qquad
1\le j\le m.
\]

Also,

\[
\Pi_V\phi_1=\phi_1.
\]

## Theorem

For every

\[
F\in\mathcal P_{\mathrm{RA1n}}^{\mathrm{terminal}},
\]

one has

\[
(I-\Pi_V)F
=
\sum_{j=1}^m c_j(F)\psi_j.
\]

Hence

\[
(I-\Pi_V)F\in W_{\perp}.
\]

## Proof

Let

\[
F\in\mathcal P_{\mathrm{RA1n}}^{\mathrm{terminal}}.
\]

By the terminal grammar hypothesis,

\[
F
=
a(F)\phi_1
+
\sum_{j=1}^m c_j(F)\psi_j.
\]

Apply \(I-\Pi_V\):

\[
(I-\Pi_V)F
=
a(F)(I-\Pi_V)\phi_1
+
\sum_{j=1}^m c_j(F)(I-\Pi_V)\psi_j.
\]

Since

\[
\Pi_V\phi_1=\phi_1,
\]

we have

\[
(I-\Pi_V)\phi_1=0.
\]

Since

\[
\psi_j\in V_{\mathrm{RA1n}}^\perp,
\]

we have

\[
\Pi_V\psi_j=0,
\]

so

\[
(I-\Pi_V)\psi_j=\psi_j.
\]

Therefore

\[
(I-\Pi_V)F
=
\sum_{j=1}^m c_j(F)\psi_j.
\]

Thus

\[
(I-\Pi_V)F\in W_{\perp}.
\]

## Consequence

By `RA1N_TRANSVERSE_RESIDUAL_FINITE_SPAN_THEOREM.md`,

\[
(I-\Pi_V)F\in W_{\perp}
\]

is the finite-span input required for the transverse Rayleigh-gap certificate chain.

## Remaining Non-Conditional Obligation

Prove from the actual RA1n terminal construction that

\[
\mathcal G_{\mathrm{terminal}}
\subset
\{\phi_1,\psi_1,\dots,\psi_m\}.
\]

Equivalently, prove that no terminal generator exists outside the finite grammar:

\[
\neg\exists \chi
\quad
\chi\in\mathcal G_{\mathrm{terminal}}
\quad
\chi\notin
\operatorname{span}\{\phi_1,\psi_1,\dots,\psi_m\}.
\]
