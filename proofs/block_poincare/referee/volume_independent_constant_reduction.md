# Volume-Independent Constant Reduction

## Finite-volume obstruction
Current bound:
\[
C_{\mathrm{bg}} \le \frac{m^2}{16},
\qquad
c_0 \ge \left(\frac14+\frac{9m^2}{32}\right)^{-1}.
\]

## Required replacement
Prove a volume-independent block-average propagation bound
\[
\sum_B |B|\,|m_B|^2
\le
C_{\mathrm{bg}}^{(\infty)}
\sum_{(B,B')\in E(\mathcal B_L)} |m_B-m_{B'}|^2,
\]
with
\[
C_{\mathrm{bg}}^{(\infty)} < \infty
\]
independent of \(m\).

## Consequent absolute constant
If such a bound holds, then
\[
C_*=\frac14+\frac92\,C_{\mathrm{bg}}^{(\infty)},
\qquad
c_0 = \left(\frac14+\frac92\,C_{\mathrm{bg}}^{(\infty)}\right)^{-1}.
\]

## Reduction target
It suffices to prove
\[
\sum_B |B|\,|m_B|^2
\le
C
\sum_{(B,B')\in E(\mathcal B_L)} |m_B-m_{B'}|^2
\]
for zero-mean block averages \(m_B\) in the infinite-volume normalization regime.

## Status
Absolute coercivity constant reduced to volume-independent block-graph Poincare bound.
